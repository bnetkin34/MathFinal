CHAIN RULE

Learn how to take a derivative using these methods!

Definition of the Chain Rule: A method used to differeniate composite functions.
Function Notation: d/dx(g(h(x))) = dg(h(x)) * dh(x)
Steps to Success:
Step 1: Differentiate the function on the outside.
Step 2: Differentiate the function on the inside.
Step 3: Simplify, if necessary.
IMPORTANT NOTE: Compare the chain rule to peeling an onion-- you peel from the outside to the inside.
