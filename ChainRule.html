CHAIN RULE

Learn how to take a derivative and apply that knowledge using these methods!

Definition of the Chain Rule: A method used to differentiate composite functions.

Function Notation: d/dx(g(h(x))) = dg(h(x)) * dh(x)

Steps to Success:

Step 1: Differentiate the outside function.
Step 2: Differentiate the inside function.
Step 3: Use the formula to simplify the equation.

IMPORTANT NOTE: Compare the chain rule to peeling an onion-- you peel from the outside to the inside.
